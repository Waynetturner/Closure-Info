> ## Overview
>
> **Power of Attorney management tool for families and elder law firms**
>
> Tags: Perfect Timing · 10x Better
>
> Every bank has its own rules for accepting Power of Attorney documents, and none of them publish those rules in advance. Specific legal language at one. A particular notarization format at another. Caregivers find out what each institution requires when the paperwork comes back rejected. Bills go unpaid. Accounts sit frozen.
>
> ConcretePOA takes the document that just got rejected and tells the caregiver exactly why. Wrong notarization format for that bank. Missing language in section three. It reformats, resubmits, and tracks approval in real time. When renewals come due, alerts go out before the deadline arrives. The rejection history becomes the product. What each bank wanted, what was missing, what finally got approved. A caregiver submitting to the same institution next month benefits from every rejection it sent last year.
>
> The first version is manual. Handle POA submissions for 20 families across three to five major banks and document every rejection, every approval requirement, every bureaucratic quirk that no website publishes. That documentation becomes the ruleset the automation runs on. Once the patterns are mapped, submission logic and verification tracking get built on top. Banks get added one at a time, prioritized by which ones cause the most rejections and serve the most elderly account holders.
>
> Caregivers pay $20 to $50 a month. Elder law firms pay $200 to $500 a month for tools that track POA status across institutions and flag renewal deadlines. Banks pay $50,000 to $500,000 annually to license the verification layer that reduces their own rejection volume. Growth starts with elder law firms, where one partnership seeds hundreds of families. Hospital discharge planners and estate attorneys feed the same pipeline. The system gets smarter with every submission. The banks that created the problem become the biggest customers.
